Privacy

Objects in Graffiti can only be found if you look in the right context. This design can be interpretted as a form of security through obscurity or as a read-only form of object capabilities : "if someone give's me a reference to the object then I can read it."

This makes context good at providing short-term privacy: if an object does not exist for very long, like a snap in Snapchat, it is unlikely for the reference to that object to travel outside it's intended audience. Context is also good at providing some amount of social privacy if you assume that other people are being polite and not going out of their way to find things they are not meant to see. However, for strict long-term privacy Graffiti also allows for a form of privacy akin to access control lists.

As we've mentioned every user has a unique "actor" URI. We can access it through $graffitiMyActor and all objects made by a person must be signed with an actor property. To make private messages we can take advantage of the bto and bcc fields.
